Please read these terms before making a booking. A quotation, booking confirmation, property-specific agreement or platform terms may contain additional terms and will take priority where it expressly differs from these general terms.

1. About these terms

These terms apply to use of our website and to accommodation supplied by Holt Accommodation Solutions Limited ("Holt", "we", "us" or "our") to a business customer, private booking customer and the people occupying the accommodation.

A "business customer" includes a company, partnership, sole trader, public body, charity or other organisation booking accommodation for business or work-related purposes.

Holt supplies accommodation as principal under its own agreement with the customer. A property may be owned by Holt or made available to Holt under a separate arrangement with its owner or provider. These website terms do not replace or amend Holt's separate agreements with landlords, hosts, property owners or other suppliers.

A person making a booking confirms that they are at least 18, have authority to bind the customer, will provide accurate occupant details and will ensure that every occupant complies with the booking terms and property rules.

2. Enquiries, quotations and binding bookings

Website content and availability information are invitations to enquire and do not constitute an offer. Bookings may be arranged directly with Holt or, for certain properties, through a third-party platform such as Airbnb.

A direct booking becomes binding when Holt receives the payment required to secure it. The booking consists of these terms, the accepted quotation or booking confirmation, any property-specific rules and any separate written agreement. For platform bookings, the platform's applicable terms also apply.

The quotation or confirmation will normally specify the property, dates, authorised occupants, price, payment schedule, deposits, included services and any cancellation or notice terms. Holt may correct an obvious pricing or clerical error before accepting payment.

3. Prices and payment

Prices, taxes and mandatory charges will be stated before a booking is concluded. Payment is normally required in advance. Depending on the booking, Holt may require monthly payments, approximately 28-day or calendar-month charging periods, one to three months in advance, a booking payment, a security deposit or another arrangement agreed in writing.

The booking confirmation or separate agreement determines the payment dates for that booking. Unless otherwise stated, invoices are payable on receipt and cleared funds must be received before occupation. Holt may withhold access, suspend services or terminate a booking for overdue payment after giving reasonable notice, except where immediate action is reasonably necessary.

The contracting customer is responsible for all sums due. Individual employees or contractors occupying corporate accommodation are not personally liable unless they separately contract with Holt or cause a loss for which the law makes them liable.

Where applicable to business customers, Holt reserves the right to charge statutory interest and reasonable debt recovery costs on overdue commercial accounts in accordance with the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998.

Holt may also recover any reasonable bank charges, chargeback fees or international payment costs incurred because of the customer's payment method or failed payment.

4. Corporate bookings

The corporate customer is responsible for its employees, contractors, visitors and other authorised occupants. It must keep Holt informed of occupant changes and may not exceed the agreed occupancy or permit occupation by anyone Holt has not approved.

Cancellation, shortening and notice rights depend on the quotation, booking confirmation or separate agreement. Unless that document permits otherwise, a fixed booking cannot be cancelled or shortened without Holt's written agreement. For a longer-term rolling booking, two months' written notice is normally required. Holt may agree a different period or a flexible extension in writing.

An early departure, temporary vacancy or change of personnel does not automatically reduce the agreed price. Extensions are subject to availability, written agreement and payment.

5. Private bookings, cancellation and no-shows

The cancellation terms shown in the quotation, booking confirmation or applicable platform listing apply. For a direct Old Rectory booking, the special terms in Schedule 1 apply.

Where a private customer cancels, Holt may retain or recover only amounts permitted by the booking terms and applicable consumer law. Holt will take reasonable steps to reduce avoidable losses, including seeking a replacement booking where reasonably practicable, and will account for net sums recovered where the law requires.

No-shows and early departures do not automatically qualify for a refund. A customer should obtain suitable travel or cancellation insurance.

6. Security deposits and customer responsibility

Where a refundable security deposit is required, Holt will return it within a reasonable period after departure once the property has been checked, less any reasonable deductions, taking account of reasonable wear and tear. Holt may require payment beyond the deposit where its reasonable loss exceeds the deposit.

  • damage, breakages, missing items or unauthorised removal of property;
  • exceptional cleaning, staining, waste removal or remediation;
  • lost keys, lock replacement, security attendance or contractor call-outs;
  • unauthorised occupants, visitors, parties, pets, smoking or commercial use;
  • substantiated noise, nuisance or antisocial behaviour and resulting reasonable costs;
  • unpaid charges or other reasonable losses caused by a breach of the booking terms;
  • additional cleaning costs where the property requires cleaning substantially beyond that reasonably expected following normal occupation;
  • replacement locks or security costs where keys are lost, not returned or security has been compromised;
  • specialist cleaning, restoration, deodorisation, upholstery treatment, pool treatment, hot tub treatment or pest-control services reasonably required as a result of the customer's actions.

Holt will consider reasonably available evidence before making a deduction, which may include photographs, inventories, invoices, staff observations, neighbour or authority reports, correspondence and readings from any properly disclosed noise-monitoring device. A complaint alone does not automatically result in forfeiture of a deposit.

7. Occupancy and conduct

Only named and authorised occupants may stay at or use the accommodation unless Holt gives prior written approval. The customer must comply with the maximum occupancy, check-in and checkout arrangements, property rules and all reasonable safety or management instructions.

  • No illegal, dangerous, threatening or antisocial activity.
  • No unauthorised parties, events, visitors or commercial activity.
  • No smoking or vaping indoors.
  • No pets unless the property listing or Holt expressly permits them; pet charges and additional rules may apply.
  • No unreasonable noise or nuisance. Occupants must respect neighbours and any property-specific quiet hours.
  • No tampering with safety, security, utility, pool, hot-tub, monitoring or access equipment.

7A. Occupant information

Holt may require the names and contact details of all occupants and may request reasonable identification where required for security, insurance, legal or regulatory purposes.

8. Check-in, access and departure

Check-in and checkout times are stated in the booking confirmation or property rules and may be varied only by prior written agreement. The customer must follow access instructions, keep keys and codes secure and return all keys when leaving.

Before departure, occupants must leave the property reasonably tidy, dispose of rubbish as directed, switch off appliances or equipment where instructed, secure the property and report damage or breakages.

8A. Deliveries

Holt accepts no responsibility for deliveries made before arrival or after departure unless expressly agreed in writing.

Deliveries accepted during a stay remain the responsibility of the customer.

9. Cleaning, linen, utilities and services

The services included in the price vary by property and booking. Cleaning, linen, utilities, internet, parking and other facilities are included only where stated. Service timings may be reasonably changed where necessary.

Internet, utilities and third-party services may occasionally be interrupted. Holt will use reasonable efforts to restore or arrange assistance but does not guarantee uninterrupted availability where a failure is outside its reasonable control.

9A. Amenities and recreational facilities

Certain properties may include amenities such as swimming pools, hot tubs, gyms, cinemas, games rooms, saunas, tennis courts, electric vehicle charging facilities, lifts, smart-home systems, outdoor kitchens or similar facilities.

Holt will use reasonable efforts to keep such amenities available but does not guarantee uninterrupted operation. Temporary unavailability caused by maintenance, repair, weather conditions, supplier failure, safety requirements or circumstances beyond Holt's reasonable control will not normally constitute a breach of contract or give rise to compensation where reasonable efforts have been made to restore availability.

Where utilities are included within the accommodation price, they are intended to cover normal domestic usage.

Holt reserves the right to recover reasonable additional costs arising from excessive, wasteful or abnormal consumption of electricity, gas, water, heating, air conditioning or EV charging facilities.

Customers and occupants must promptly report faults, accidents, damage, loss, safety concerns and complaints, and must give Holt a reasonable opportunity to investigate and remedy them. They must not arrange repairs or alter the property without Holt's approval except where urgently necessary to prevent immediate harm.

Holt, a property owner, housekeeper or authorised contractor may enter on reasonable notice for inspection, cleaning, maintenance, compliance or safety purposes. Entry may be made without notice in a genuine emergency, where serious damage is suspected or where reasonably necessary to protect people or property.

10A. Maintenance and contractor attendance

Customers acknowledge that maintenance contractors, utility providers, cleaners, inspectors and other authorised personnel may occasionally require access during a stay.

Holt will provide reasonable notice wherever practicable, except in emergencies or where urgent works are required.

11. Serious breaches and removal

Holt may refuse access, require occupants to leave or terminate a booking where reasonably necessary because of non-payment, illegal activity, serious nuisance, danger, significant damage, unauthorised occupation or a serious or repeated breach of the terms. No refund will be due for accommodation lost because of the customer's breach, subject always to applicable law. The customer remains responsible for Holt's reasonable resulting losses.

11A. Future bookings

Holt reserves the right to decline future bookings from any customer or occupant who has previously breached booking terms, caused significant nuisance, damage or risk, or otherwise behaved in a manner reasonably considered unsuitable.

12. Property availability and substitution

If booked accommodation becomes unavailable or materially unsuitable, Holt may offer reasonably comparable alternative accommodation. Holt will consider location, capacity, standard and the purpose of the stay, although an exact match cannot be guaranteed.

Any alternative accommodation may be operated by Holt Accommodation Solutions Limited or by another carefully selected accommodation provider engaged by Holt.

If Holt cannot provide a reasonable alternative, or the customer reasonably declines it, Holt will refund amounts paid for accommodation not provided. This is without prejudice to any further rights that cannot legally be excluded.

Holt is not responsible for delay or failure caused by events outside its reasonable control, including severe weather, flooding, fire, utility or communications failure, water shortages, utility rationing, contamination events, mandatory conservation measures, emergency repairs, epidemic, government action, transport disruption or acts of a property owner or third-party supplier that Holt could not reasonably prevent.

Holt will take reasonable steps to minimise disruption and, where appropriate, offer an alternative, credit or refund for accommodation it cannot provide. Nothing in this clause removes rights that cannot lawfully be excluded.

Customers are responsible for ensuring that they maintain any insurance appropriate to their stay, including travel insurance, cancellation insurance, public liability insurance and insurance for personal possessions, tools, equipment or business assets where relevant.

Holt processes personal information in accordance with its Privacy Notice and may share relevant information with property owners, corporate customers, insurers, cleaners, maintenance contractors, booking platforms, payment providers, professional advisers and public authorities where lawful and necessary.

THE OLD RECTORY

Additional direct-booking terms and property rules

S1. Direct booking payments

A direct booking requires a £50 per person booking payment. It is credited towards the total accommodation price and reserves the dates once received. The remaining balance is due eight weeks before arrival.

If the guest cancels, the £50 per person booking payment is non-refundable to the fullest extent permitted by law. If cancellation occurs within eight weeks of arrival, the full accommodation price remains payable and is non-refundable, subject to Holt crediting any net sum recovered from a replacement booking where applicable law requires. If Holt cancels because it cannot provide the accommodation or a reasonable alternative, payments for accommodation not provided will be refunded.

S2. Security deposits

A refundable security deposit of £40 per person is due four weeks before arrival. An additional refundable security deposit of £40 per person applies to an approved hen or stag booking, making total security deposits of £80 per person for those groups.

Security deposits are returned after the stay once checks are completed, less reasonable deductions under clause 6. A noise complaint will be investigated and does not automatically result in a deduction.

S3. Arrival, departure and authorised guests

Normal check-in is between 4:00 pm and 6:00 pm and checkout is by 10:00 am. Different arrangements require Holt's prior written agreement.

Only booked and named guests may enter or stay unless Holt agrees otherwise in advance. Additional overnight guests may be charged at a pre-agreed rate. Hen, stag and similar group bookings require prior approval and details of the planned gathering.

S4. Noise and neighbours

Quiet hours are 9:00 pm to 8:00 am unless Holt agrees a variation in writing. No outdoor music is permitted after 9:00 pm.

Guests must ensure that noise outside the property is kept to a level that cannot reasonably be heard by neighbouring properties after 9:00 pm.

Reasonable indoor music and social activity are permitted provided they cannot be heard outside and do not cause nuisance. Guests must respond promptly to any request from Holt or the housekeeper to reduce noise.

S5. Property-specific rules

  • Pets are permitted subject to the booking confirmation and any stated conditions.
  • No smoking or vaping indoors and absolutely no fireworks.
  • Fake tan must be thoroughly removed before using the pool or hot tub because permanent staining may be charged to the customer.
  • Grocery delivery drivers must be given the guest's phone number and gate code and must not use the owners' buzzer.
  • The housekeeper lives on site at the bottom of the drive and may come and go while respecting guest privacy.
  • Guests must follow the supplied checkout instructions for towels, rubbish, keys, security, pool equipment, outdoor furniture, dishes and appliances.
  • Disposable barbecues are not permitted anywhere on the property unless expressly authorised.
  • Only toilet paper may be flushed. Guests will be responsible for the reasonable costs of clearing blockages caused by wipes, sanitary products, nappies, fats, oils, food waste or other unsuitable items.
  • Where a property operates a private drainage or septic system, guests must follow all instructions relating to suitable products and waste disposal.

S5A. Electric vehicle charging

Electric vehicles may only be charged using any designated EV charging equipment provided at the property. Charging from domestic sockets, extension leads or any unauthorised electrical outlet is prohibited unless Holt has given prior written permission. Guests are responsible for any damage resulting from unauthorised charging.

S5B. Swimming pool and hot tub

The swimming pool and hot tub may occasionally be unavailable because of maintenance, servicing, water quality management, weather conditions or emergency repair. Holt will use reasonable endeavours to minimise disruption, but temporary unavailability shall not normally give rise to compensation where reasonable steps have been taken to restore the facilities.

S5C. Weddings, events and commercial activities

Weddings, receptions, corporate events, filming, photography sessions, retreats, workshops or other organised events are not permitted unless expressly agreed in writing.

Additional charges, deposits, insurance requirements and event-specific terms may apply.

S5D. Drones

Use of drones or similar unmanned aerial devices at or over the property requires Holt's prior written approval and must comply with all applicable laws and regulations.

S5E. Biosecurity

Guests must not introduce hazardous materials, illegal substances, livestock, agricultural products, invasive species or any item likely to create contamination, infestation or environmental risk.

S6. Facilities and safety information

The property includes stairs, a swimming pool, hot tub, large trampoline and play equipment. Guests must supervise children and vulnerable persons, follow all safety instructions and sign any reasonable pre-arrival acknowledgement relating to these facilities. Use is at the guest's own risk, but this does not exclude Holt's liability where it cannot legally be excluded.

Children must be supervised by a responsible adult at all times when using or near the swimming pool, hot tub, trampoline or play equipment.

Guests must not use the swimming pool or hot tub while under the excessive influence of alcohol, drugs or medication that may impair judgement or safety.

Exterior security cameras cover entrances, driveways, access points and public areas around the swimming pool. They are used for safety and security and are not located in private indoor areas.

Smoke alarms are installed. Current safety and alarm information supplied before or at arrival forms part of the property information. Guests must immediately report any fault or concern and must not disable or interfere with safety equipment.
